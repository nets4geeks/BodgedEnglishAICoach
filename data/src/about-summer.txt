The Summer

Summer arrives like an old friend, bringing long days and a slower [pace] of life. In many countries, the season is defined by [heatwave]s, afternoon [thunderstorm]s, and the smell of barbecue drifting from neighbouring gardens. While some people welcome the warmth, others find the humidity unbearable, especially in cities where concrete traps the heat.
For students, summer means a break from deadlines, but for farmers, it is the most [demanding time] of the year. Early mornings are the best moment to be outside, before the sun reaches its peak and the air shimmers with heat. Many families head to the coast, where sea breezes offer relief and the rhythm of waves replaces the noise of traffic.
However, climate change is making summers more extreme. Heatwaves last longer, [wildfire]s spread faster, and droughts threaten water supplies. As a result, air conditioning sales have risen sharply, and cities are planting more trees to create shade. Despite these challenges, there is still something magical about a summer evening – the lingering light, the sound of children playing, and the promise of a gentle night ahead.
